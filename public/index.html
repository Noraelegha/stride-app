The problem

You don't have a motivation problem. You have a direction problem.

Everyone says "put yourself out there." But nobody tells you what that actually means. What to do today. What to do first. So you try everything, get overwhelmed, and stop.

"I'll start on Monday."

The goal is still there. Monday just keeps moving further away.

"I just need to get organised first."

Organisation is the comfortable feeling of not actually starting.

"I know what to do. I just don't do it."

You don't need more information. You need someone to make the first step impossible to avoid.

"I've tried apps. They don't stick."

Most apps give you a better list. Stride gives you a push every single day.

How it works

One goal. One task. Every day.

01

Tell Dash your goal

Not a vague intention a real goal. "Land 3 consulting clients." "Finish my book." "Relocate to London." Dash maps the route so you don't have to.

02

Get one micro-task daily

Every morning, one task. Under 5 minutes. No research required. Small enough that you can't say no to it big enough that it actually moves the needle.

03

Dash keeps you honest

Miss a task and Dash notices. Go quiet and Dash shows up. Your streak is on the line and Dash takes that personally. The goal doesn't build itself, but it does build one task at a time.

Meet Dash

Not a chatbot. Your accountability engine.

Dash learns how you work. It knows when you're avoiding the task. It knows when you need a push versus when you need a smaller door. It remembers what you said your Big Prize was and it holds you to it.

Level 1 Dash is your cheerleader. Level 3 Dash is knocking on the glass of your phone screen. You choose which one shows up based entirely on whether you showed up first.
